Who We Are

Master Students in AI for Internet of Things

We are a team of passionate master's degree students in Artificial Intelligence for Internet of Things at University Abdelhamid Ibn Badis Mostaganem. As founders of the Cod'In Club, we're dedicated to applying cutting-edge technology to solve real-world environmental challenges.

Our project, DesertCool, aims to address the extreme heat conditions in desert cities like Adrar using sustainable, AI-driven solutions that combine renewable energy, water recycling, and smart grid technology.

Our Integrated Solutions

Solar Panel System

High-efficiency solar panels convert abundant desert sunlight into clean electricity, providing power for cooling systems and reducing reliance on fossil fuels.

Output: 1,850 to 2,100 kWh per hour

Water Recycling

Advanced treatment processes recycle wastewater for cooling applications, reducing freshwater consumption by up to 70% in industrial and residential settings.

Wind Turbines

Medium-scale wind turbines (100-500KW capacity) harness desert winds to generate additional renewable energy, complementing solar power during low-light periods.

Misting System

High-pressure misting systems reduce ambient temperature by up to 30°C through evaporative cooling, with integrated air purification and dust control features.

Strategic Planting

Drought-resistant native species (Acacia, Tamarisk, Eucalyptus) provide natural shade, reduce ground temperature, and combat desertification.

AI Smart Grid

Intelligent energy management system optimizes power distribution, predicts demand, and integrates renewable sources for maximum efficiency and reliability.
